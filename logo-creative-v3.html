Brand Identity v3 — Conceptually Grounded

ReachMasked

Each logo concept is derived directly from what the product does: a stranger scans your vehicle's tag to contact you — privately, without exposing a single number.

Concept A · The Phantom Tag
The Physical Tag
Made Digital
The product's most literal physical object — the NFC/QR sticker tag placed on a vehicle — rendered as the logo mark itself. The tag is hauntingly transparent (the mask), with glowing QR-like data inside and live NFC signal arcs radiating outward (the reach). You immediately know: "tap or scan this."
Product-Literal NFC / QR Metaphor Glassmorphic

Concept B · The Eye of Privacy
Always Watching.
Never Exposed.
An eye — the universal symbol of awareness, watching, reaching. But the iris is overlaid with live scan lines, a visual "masking" of identity. The eye sees everything, but its own identity is obscured. The animated scan lines breathe life into the mark and convey active, real-time privacy monitoring.
Highly Symbolic Animated Awareness + Privacy

Concept C · The Pixelated Plate
Your Car, Contactable.
Your Plate, Hidden.
A sleek vehicle icon where the license plate area is deliberately pixelated like a blur/mask — referencing both data censoring and the privacy core of the product. A scan-signal arc above the roof represents the QR/NFC tap event. This logo literally answers: "What does ReachMasked do?" with a single image.
Vehicle-Literal Pixelated Privacy Most Explanatory

Concept D · The Relay Node
The Signal.
The Shield.
The Service.
The defining concept. Two anonymous silhouettes (the stranger and the owner) pass a live signal through a glowing central relay node. The relay node — the ReachMasked product itself — bears a mask symbol. It says in one diagram: "We are the privacy-preserving relay between two parties who should never have to share their numbers." The orbital rings on the relay node suggest always-on, always-safe.
Story-Led Animated Live Signal Most Defining
